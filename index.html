3:00 AM

☀️ Wake Up & Morning Prayer

Home / Hotel

Start your wedding day the best way possible — with Jehovah. Wake up early, say a heartfelt prayer together or individually, asking for His blessing, peace, and guidance throughout the day.

Morning Routine:

  • 3:00 AM — Wake up. Take a moment of quiet reflection. Read a comforting scripture together (suggestion: Ecclesiastes 4:9-12 or Proverbs 18:22).
  • 3:15 AM — Personal prayer to Jehovah. Thank Him for this day. Ask for calmness and joy.
  • 3:30 AM — Freshen up, shower, and prepare for the HMUA team.
💎 Bride & Groom: Don’t skip this. Starting the day with Jehovah will set the right tone for everything. Philippians 4:6, 7 reminds us: “Do not be anxious over anything, but in everything by prayer and supplication along with thanksgiving, let your petitions be made known to God; and the peace of God that surpasses all understanding will guard your hearts.”
3:30 AM

🍴 Breakfast

Home / Hotel

Eat a good, balanced breakfast before the HMUA arrives. This is essential — you won’t have time to eat properly again until the reception dinner!

Breakfast Tips:

  • Eat something substantial — eggs, toast, oatmeal, fruit, or a rice meal. You need energy for a long day.
  • Stay hydrated — drink water or juice. Avoid too much coffee (can cause jitters and dehydration).
  • Avoid messy/greasy foods — you don’t want stains before HMUA starts!
  • Bride: Use a straw for drinks once makeup begins. Have mints or breath spray ready.
  • Groom: Eat well! You may be more anxious than you think. A good meal settles the nerves.
💎 Bride & Groom: Do NOT skip breakfast. It’s going to be a very long and exciting day. Your body and mind need fuel. Have someone prepare it for you so you can relax.
4:00 AM

💄 HMUA Session (Hair & Makeup)

Home / Hotel

The HMUA (Hair and Makeup Artist) arrives. The Bride’s session takes about 3–4 hours; the Groom’s grooming takes about 30 minutes–1 hour.

HMUA Schedule:

  • 4:00 – 4:30 AM — HMUA team sets up. Bride sits in a comfortable spot with good lighting.
  • 4:30 – 6:00 AM — Bride’s base makeup, contouring, and eye makeup.
  • 6:00 – 7:00 AM — Bride’s hair styling, veil placement, and final touches.
  • 7:00 – 7:30 AM — Groom’s grooming: light makeup, hair styling, and outfit check.
  • 7:30 AM — Both ready for the photo/video coverage.
HMUA Reminders
  • Make sure the HMUA has the correct shades and products discussed during trials.
  • Bride: Wear a button-down top so you don’t mess up hair/makeup when changing into your gown.
  • Groom: Have your barong or suit ready to put on after grooming.
  • Keep a touch-up kit ready (powder, lipstick, hairpins) to bring to the venue.
  • Have drinking water with a straw available for the Bride during the session.
💎 Bride: Relax and enjoy being pampered. This is your time. Put on calming music, maybe some Kingdom Songs to keep the right spirit.
💎 Groom: Use this time to finalize your personal vows or speech if you haven’t, or simply relax and read an encouraging Bible passage.
9:00 AM

🎥 Photo & Video Coverage Begins

Home / Hotel

The photo and video team arrives to capture the getting-ready moments — some of the most precious shots of the day.

Photo/Video Session:

  • 9:00 – 9:30 AM — Getting-Ready Shots: Bride putting on veil, shoes, jewelry. Groom adjusting tie/barong. Close-up details of rings, bouquet, invitation, Bible.
  • 9:30 – 10:00 AM — Solo Portraits: Bride and Groom individual portraits (separate rooms or areas).
  • 10:00 – 10:30 AM — First Look / Couple Feature: Optional first-look moment between Bride & Groom (if desired). Couple photos together.
  • 10:30 – 11:00 AM — Family & Entourage Shots: Quick group photos with parents, witnesses, and close family.
  • 11:00 – 11:30 AM — Wrap-up & travel preparation. Final touch-ups.
Photo/Video Tips
  • Prepare all your accessories (shoes, jewelry, rings, bouquet, Bible) neatly on a table for detail shots.
  • Choose a room with good natural lighting for the best photos.
  • Groom: Have someone help you with cufflinks/tie for natural candid moments.
  • Bride: Have your maid of honor help with the veil — it creates beautiful photos.
  • Trust your photographer! They know the best angles and poses.
💎 Bride & Groom: These “getting-ready” photos will be some of your favorites! Just be natural and enjoy the process. The photo/video team knows what they’re doing. The SDE video will pull from these beautiful moments too.
11:30 AM

🚗 Travel to Ceremony Venue

In Transit

Leave for the ceremony venue. Plan to arrive by 12:00 PM at the latest. Account for traffic, parking, and settling in.

💎 Bride & Groom: Say a short prayer together before leaving. Ask Jehovah to bless the ceremony and to help you both feel His presence. Bring your touch-up kit, water, mints, and a light snack (granola bar) just in case.
12:00 PM

Preparations & Ingress

Ceremony Venue

Arrive at the venue at least 2 hours before the ceremony, already dressed for the occasion.

Behind-the-Scenes Details
  • AV Operators — Test sound system & check music sequence: interlude, wedding march, opening song (Song #36), signing music (Song #87), pictorial music.
  • AV/Stage Attendants — Set stage per Ceremony Layout (podium, mic stand, 2 microphones, signing table). Prepare 3 additional chairs near stage for contract signing. Turn ON air conditioning.
  • Receptionists/Gift Custodians — Set place/name cards per seating arrangement. Secure gift area. Distribute handout songs to guests.
  • Welcome Party — Ready at entrances.
  • Door Keepers — Unlock all exits, verify emergency signs visible, clear pathways.
  • Transport/Parking Attendants — Confirm all VIP vehicles and drivers are available.
  • Entourage Coordinators — Secure flowers for entourage & bridal car. Confirm Bible and signing pens on table. Begin assembling entourage at holding area.
💎 Bride & Groom: Just get ready and relax! Your coordinators, AV team, and door keepers are handling all the setup. Focus on looking and feeling your best.

Guests Arrive & Entourage Assembly

Behind-the-Scenes Details
  • Welcome Party & Receptionists — Warmly welcome guests, especially direct those with assigned seats.
  • Entourage Coordinators — Confirm all entourage participants are complete. Pin/give flowers and remind them of seats, marching sequence, and route.
💎 Bride & Groom: Stay in the holding area. The coordinators will come to you with updates. Deep breaths — everything is going smoothly!

Ceremony Venue

  • Entourage Coordinator 2 — Assembles Best Men, Matrons of Honor, and Maid of Honor at marker L2.
  • Entourage Coordinator 3 — Stations with photo/video team to coordinate coverage.
  • Receptionists & Welcome Party — Clear hallways & aisles, request audience to be seated.
💎 Bride & Groom: Your coordinator will position you at your marker. Just follow their lead and remember to smile!
1:55 PM

Final Countdown

Ceremony Venue

The Groom gets into marching position. Music stops for the Chairman's announcements.

Behind-the-Scenes Details

  • AV/Stage Attendants — Remind Sound Operators that ceremony is about to start. Stop music.
  • Door Keepers 2 — Close doors at auxiliary hall. Place sign "Please use the main door."
  • Chairman — Begins announcements.
💎 Groom: This is your moment to get into position. Stay calm, stand tall, and get ready to walk!
💎 Bride: Wait for your cue. The dramatic entrance is all yours!
2:00 PM

🎈 Ceremony Begins — Wedding March!

Ceremony Venue

The moment you've been waiting for! The wedding march music plays, the entourage processes in, and the Chairman welcomes everyone.

Wedding March Sequence:

  1. Chairman begins welcome remarks & announces entrance of entourage
  2. Director cues AV Operators — wedding march music plays
  3. Best Men, Matrons of Honor, and Maid of Honor enter
  4. Chairman announces entrance of the Bride
  5. Dramatic & suspenseful entrance of the Bride
  6. Chairman's introduction to the Wedding Talk
  7. Opening Song (Song #36)
Behind-the-Scenes Details
  • Door Keepers 1 — Close doors during march. Hold back latecomers until march is over.
  • Aisle Keepers — In position, guiding marchers on route, reminding them to stop & smile. Tactfully keeping audience seated.
  • Receptionists — Positioned front right side to guide entourage to seats.
  • Director — Coordinates all cues between teams.
💎 Bride & Groom: Walk slowly, enjoy the moment, and look at each other! The aisle keepers will guide you. Don't rush — everyone is there to see YOU. Pay attention to the aisle keepers for stop-and-smile cues for great photos.
~2:30 PM

Wedding Talk & Vows

Ceremony Venue

The Bible-based wedding talk proceeds — a beautiful, dignified message about Jehovah’s purpose for marriage. The couple listens on stage as the speaker shares Scriptural counsel for a happy, God-honoring marriage.

Behind-the-Scenes Details
  • Door Keepers 1 — Make headcount and inform wedding director.
  • Receptionists & Welcome Party — Minimize disturbances (phones, children, etc.)
  • Director & Entourage Coordinators — Attentive to needs of Bridal Entourage & VIPs. Make sure newlyweds look their best.
💎 Bride & Groom: Listen carefully to the Scriptural counsel — it’s Jehovah’s guidance for your marriage. These words will be a foundation you can return to for years. Your team is handling everything around you.
~2:45 PM

Contract Signing

Ceremony Venue

The official marriage contract is signed by the couple, witnesses, and officiating minister.

Signing Seating (facing audience):

Female Witness Male Witness Officiating Minister Groom Bride

Reverse-V formation facing the audience with the center table at their midst.

Behind-the-Scenes Details
  • Chairman — Announces contract signing and reminders.
  • Sound — Plays music during signing (Song #87).
  • AV/Stage Attendant — Sets up 3 additional chairs in reverse-V formation. Clears center table for signing.
  • After signing: Chairs removed, stage prepared for pictorial.
  • Officiating Minister — After contract signing: song, then prayer.
  • AV Operators — Plays closing song.
💎 Bride & Groom: Follow the minister’s and chairman’s instructions for signing. The stage attendants will guide you to your chairs. This is the moment you become officially married — a covenant made before Jehovah!

Wedding Pictorial (Ceremony Venue)

Ceremony Venue

Time for photos! The Pictorial Emcee will announce the newlyweds and guide through the photo sequence.

Behind-the-Scenes Details

💎 Bride & Groom: Smile and enjoy the photos! Your coordinators will tell you who to pose with. Receptionists leave first for the reception venue — you will be the last to leave.

Behind-the-Scenes Details

Final Reception Preparations

Two minutes before the reception program starts. The newlyweds prepare for their grand entrance.

~5:00 PM

Entertainment Program

Reception Venue

A simple and dignified program that celebrates your marriage — with heartfelt performances, your first dance, and a beautiful video recap of the day.

Complete Program Flow:

  1. Emcees’ Welcome & Opening Remarks — Sets the warm, dignified tone for the program
  2. Growing Up Video — A heartwarming video featuring the Bride & Groom’s journey from childhood to this beautiful day
  3. Performer 1 — Song 1 of 2 — First performer sings their first song
  4. Performer 1 — Song 2 of 2 — First performer sings their second song
  5. Performer 2 — Song 1 of 2 — Second performer sings their first song
  6. Performer 2 — Song 2 of 2 — Second performer sings their second song
  7. Newlyweds’ First Dance — The Bride & Groom share their first dance as a married couple
  8. Performer 3 — Song 1 of 2 — Third performer sings their first song
  9. Performer 3 — Song 2 of 2 — Third performer sings their second song
  10. Newlyweds’ Speech & Thank You’s — Express gratitude to Jehovah, parents, family, friends, and the hardworking team
  11. SDE (Same-Day Edit) Video — A cinematic recap of the entire wedding day from morning preparations to the reception
Behind-the-Scenes Details
  • Director — Cues Reception Emcees to begin program. Manages transitions between each segment.
  • AV/Stage Attendants — Always alert for sound quality, microphone handoffs, and video playback. Test all performer tracks and video files beforehand.
  • Entourage Coordinator 3 — Check with Photo/Video team if SDE and Growing Up Video are ready and loaded.
  • Performers — 3 performers, each singing 2 songs. Songs should be dignified and appropriate for the occasion.
  • Important: All entertainment should be simple, dignified, and appropriate — reflecting well on the couple and giving glory to Jehovah’s name.
💎 Bride & Groom: The Growing Up Video will be emotional — enjoy it! When it’s time for your first dance, don’t worry about being perfect — just be present with each other. For your speech, thank Jehovah first, then your parents and loved ones. The SDE is a wonderful surprise to end the program — sit back and relive your beautiful day!

💎 Bride & Groom: Enjoy this time with your guests! Thank Jehovah in your heart for a beautiful day. When you’re ready to leave, your transport team will have everything arranged. Congratulations — you did it! May Jehovah bless your marriage richly.

Team Roles & Responsibilities

Every team member contributes to making this day perfect. Here's who does what.

Wedding Director

Both

The main coordinator who cues all teams. Controls the flow of the entire program from ceremony through reception.

  • Cues AV operators, emcees, door keepers
  • Coordinates all transitions
  • Manages timing of events

Entourage Coordinators

Both
  • EC1 — Assembles Bride, Groom, Parents & Witnesses

  • At reception: manage newlyweds table, cake, presidential table

AV Operators

Both
  • Test & manage sound system

  • Check donation boxes after ceremony

AV/Stage Attendants

Both

Tips for the Bride & Groom

Practical reminders to help you stay calm, keep focused, and give glory to Jehovah.

📖

Start with Jehovah

Begin your day in prayer. Ask Jehovah for peace, calmness, and joy. Read Philippians 4:6, 7 together. He will guard your hearts.

🍴

Eat a Good Breakfast

Don’t skip it! Eat by 3:30 AM before HMUA starts. Eggs, toast, fruit — you need energy for a very long day.

💧

Stay Hydrated

Keep water nearby all day. Use a straw after makeup. Avoid excess coffee — water and juice are your friends.

💄

Trust Your HMUA

Wear a button-down top to your session. Relax, listen to calming music, and let the artist work. You’ll look amazing.

📷

Enjoy the Photos

The getting-ready shots are some of the most precious. Be natural, follow the photographer’s lead, and smile from the heart.

😌

Breathe & Be Present

Take deep breaths. The team handles logistics. Your only job: enjoy the day, each other, and Jehovah’s blessing.

💃

Walk Slowly

During the march and grand entrance — take it slow. Look at each other. The aisle keepers will guide you.

💕

Keep It Dignified

Simple and dignified — that’s the theme. Your speech, dance, and program all reflect well on Jehovah’s name.

🙏

Thank Jehovah First

In your speech, acknowledge Jehovah’s role in bringing you together. Then thank your parents, family, and the team.

📱

Phone on Silent

Hand your phone to a trusted person. Stay unplugged and fully present. The photo/video team will capture everything.
